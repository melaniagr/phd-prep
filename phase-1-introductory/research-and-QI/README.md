#### 4. Incisional Negative Pressure Wound Dressings (iNPWD) for Spinal Fusions
**Role:** Led full project independently  
**Supervisor:** Mr. Mohit Arora, Consultant Neurosurgeon  
**Scope:** Comprehensive literature review + local audit of iNPWD outcomes in spinal fusion procedures

Abstract:

**Background:** While NICE-approved guidance exists for iNPWD use in colorectal, cardiothoracic, vascular, and orthopedic surgery, there is no clear guidance for spinal surgery. Use is based on surgeon preference rather than evidence-based indications.

**Methods:**
- **Literature Review:** Systematic review of published evidence on iNPWD in spinal fusion procedures
- **Retrospective Audit:** Analysis of 182 consecutive spinal fusion cases (July 2021 - July 2023) at ARI
  - Identified 15 cases where iNPWD was used (13 prophylactically)
  - Compared to 13 demographically matched controls without iNPWD
  - Data sources: inpatient notes, discharge letters, outpatient follow-up records

**Key Findings:**
- **iNPWD group (n=13):** 0% surgical site infection (SSI), normal wound healing in all cases
- **Control group (n=13):** 15.4% SSI rate (2/13 patients)
- **Implication:** Potential benefit of prophylactic iNPWD in reducing SSI in spinal fusion procedures

**Conclusions & Recommendations:**
- iNPWD shows promise for spinal fusion procedures but requires further investigation
- Need for prospective studies to establish clear indications and guidelines
- Potential for cost savings and improved patient outcomes with wider implementation

**Impact:** Project escalated to **national presentation at Scottish Spine Surgeons 14th Annual Meeting** (2024), reaching senior surgeons and peers across Scotland. Contributed to emerging evidence base for iNPWD use in spinal surgery.

**Deliverables:** Literature review, audit protocol, data analysis, presentation slides, departmental report
